## Create Your Account

Setting up your JFrog Fly account is the first step to unified artifact management.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign Up">
    Navigate to [fly.jfrog.ai](https://fly.jfrog.ai) and click **Get Started**.
    
    You can sign up using:
    - **GitHub** - Recommended for seamless repository integration
    - **GitLab** - For GitLab-centric workflows
    - **Email** - Traditional email and password registration
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Choose Your Subdomain">
    Select a unique subdomain for your Fly Registry:
    
    ```
    <your-team-name>.jfrog.io
    ```
    
    <Warning>
      Your subdomain cannot be changed after creation. Choose carefully!
    </Warning>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Verify Your Email">
    If you signed up with email, check your inbox for a verification link and click to activate your account.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Team Configuration

### Create Your Team

After account creation, you'll be prompted to set up your team workspace:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Team Name" icon="users" defaultOpen>
    Choose a descriptive name for your team or organization. This will be visible to all team members.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Team Settings" icon="gear">
    Configure default settings for your team:
    - **Default visibility** for new repositories
    - **Retention policies** for artifacts
    - **Security settings** and access controls
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Invite Team Members

Grow your team by inviting collaborators:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Email Invitation">
    1. Go to **Settings** → **Team Members**
    2. Click **Invite Member**
    3. Enter their email address
    4. Select their role:
       - **Admin** - Full access to all features
       - **Developer** - Can publish and manage artifacts
       - **Viewer** - Read-only access
    5. Click **Send Invitation**
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Link Sharing">
    1. Go to **Settings** → **Team Members**
    2. Click **Generate Invite Link**
    3. Set link expiration (optional)
    4. Share the link with your team
    
    <Note>
      Users joining via link will have the default "Developer" role. Admins can adjust roles after they join.
    </Note>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Your Fly Registry URL

Once your account is set up, your registry URL will be:

<CodeGroup>
```bash Registry URL
https://<your-subdomain>.jfrog.io
```

```bash Example
https://acme-corp.jfrog.io
```
</CodeGroup>

This URL is used for:
- Pushing and pulling artifacts
- Configuring package managers
- API access and integrations

## Account Security

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Two-Factor Authentication" icon="shield-halved">
    Enable 2FA in **Settings** → **Security** for additional account protection.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Access Tokens" icon="key">
    Generate access tokens in **Settings** → **Tokens** for CLI and CI/CD access.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Creating Access Tokens

Access tokens are required for programmatic access to your Fly Registry:

```bash
# Token format
fly_<token_id>_<token_secret>
```

<Warning>
  Store your access tokens securely. They cannot be retrieved after creation - only regenerated.
</Warning>
